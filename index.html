Core Values

Demetra tells you what you need to hear, not what you want to hear. If your numbers are concerning, we say so clearly. No alarmism to drive engagement. No sugarcoating. Trust is built on truth.
Every recommendation traces back to published clinical guidelines: USPSTF, AHA, ACC, ADA. We do not invent health advice. We translate what the best physicians already know. When the evidence is uncertain, we say that too.
Your father's diagnosis changes what you should be screening for. Your child's vaccine schedule matters to the whole household. We build for the family unit because that is how health actually works.
We use highly-evidenced clinical logic that is auditable and versioned. We do not let language models improvise with your health. But we also do not hide behind perfectionism: we ship, we learn, we improve. Every week.
We do not sell it. We do not monetize it. We do not share it without your explicit permission. Your health information exists to serve you and the people you choose to share it with. Full stop.
The free tier is not a teaser. It delivers the full standard of care for prevention. We believe everyone deserves to know their risks and what to do about them, regardless of income.
We exist to make your relationship with your doctor better, not to replace it. Better data going in means better conversations, better decisions, and better outcomes. We strengthen the care team, we do not bypass it.

Why Demetra?

Growing up in a family of doctors in Cyprus, I watched my mother do something that no app, no hospital, and no health system does well. She paid attention. She remembered that my uncle's cholesterol was high last year and followed up. She noticed when my grandfather's blood pressure crept up before he did. She caught things early because she cared continuously, not just when someone walked into a clinic.

That's not a medical superpower. It's just what happens when someone who understands medicine also loves you.

The problem is that most people don't have a doctor in the family. And the healthcare system was never designed to fill that gap. It waits for you to get sick, treats you when you do, and sends you home to figure out the rest on your own. Millions of people are walking around with high blood pressure, pre-diabetes, or missed medication needs and nobody is telling them.

I left a career building clinical AI at Stanford and Google because I realized the bottleneck in preventive care isn't data and it isn't technology. It's that nobody is applying clinical reasoning to the data that already exists, proactively, for the people who need it most. Your age, your family history, your last blood test: that's often enough to know what you should be doing. But nobody is connecting those dots for you.

Demetra is a doctor, a mother, and the person her whole family calls when something feels off. Not everyone has a Demetra. Now you do. We built this platform so every family has someone watching over their health with the same knowledge, persistence, and love.
